About Nipsey

Nipsey Hussle was born on August 15, 1985 in Los Angeles, California. He was raised in the Crenshaw neighborhood of South Los Angeles with his brother Samiel and his sister Samantha. Nipsey attended Hamilton High School in the nearby Castle Heights neighborhood, but dropped out before graduating. At the age of 14, Nipsey left home and joined the local Rollin 60s Neighborhood Crips, a sub-group of the larger Crips gang primarily based in his home neighborhood of Crenshaw. His stage name, a play on the name of comedian Nipsey Russell, originated as a nickname given to him by a childhood friend. His name was Ermias Joseph Asghedom. He died on March 31, 2019 at age of 33, after a shooting in Los Angeles near a clothing store he owned. Nipsey died because of researching and filming a documentary about Doctor Sebi and his trial about pharmacy crime. Grammy Nominated

The discography of Nipsey Hussle consists of one studio album, 49 singles (including 19 as a featured artist), three compilation albums and 13 mixtapes. He has released numerous mixtapes like "Slauson boy", "The Marathon", "The Marathon Continues", "Crenshaw", "Slauson boy 2", "Bullets ain't got no name vol. 1", "Bullets ain't got no name vol. 2", "Bullets ain't got no name vol. 3". There was this one new album released on February 16, 2018 called "Victory Lap". When the 2019 Grammy nominations were announced on Friday morning, there was "Victory Lap", the latest album from L.A. veteran Nipsey Hussle. At that moment, Nipsey received a reward for The Best Rap Album at the 61st Grammy Awards, which was his biggest success in music.
